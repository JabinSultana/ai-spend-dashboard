# User Interviews

## Interview 1

Name / Initials:
A.R.

Role / Company Stage:
Freelance Frontend Developer — Solo Freelancer

### Key Quotes

- "I pay for both ChatGPT Plus and Claude Pro, but honestly I probably don’t use both enough every month."
- "Most freelancers don’t even track AI expenses separately from other software subscriptions."
- "If a tool could quickly show where I’m wasting money without making me sign up first, I’d try it."

### Most Surprising Insight

The interviewee said they often subscribe to multiple AI tools “just in case” without evaluating overlap between features or actual usage frequency.

### What Changed in the Product

This conversation reinforced the decision to avoid forcing login before showing audit results. I also focused more on showing simple optimization suggestions instead of overwhelming technical analysis.

## Interview 2

Name / Initials:
S.K.

Role / Company Stage:
Computer Science Student — Small Side Project Team

### Key Quotes

- "Our team shares AI accounts sometimes because paying individually for every tool gets expensive."
- "We started with ChatGPT, then added Gemini because of free credits, and now nobody knows which tool we use most."
- "I’d trust the recommendations more if the reasoning was transparent instead of just saying ‘switch tools.’"

### Most Surprising Insight

The interviewee cared more about understanding _why_ a recommendation was made than the actual savings amount itself.

### What Changed in the Product

I improved the audit recommendation logic to include short finance-style explanations rather than generic cost-cutting messages. I also added more transparent savings reasoning inside the audit results.

## Interview 3

Name / Initials:
M.P.

Role / Company Stage:
Startup Marketing Associate — Early-Stage Startup

### Key Quotes

- "Different departments buy different AI subscriptions without coordination."
- "We probably spend more on unused AI subscriptions than we realize."
- "A shareable audit report would actually be useful internally because finance teams always ask for screenshots and summaries."

### Most Surprising Insight

The interviewee viewed the shareable audit feature as more valuable for internal team discussions than for social sharing.

### What Changed in the Product

This feedback increased the priority of the shareable report feature. I focused more on making the audit results visually clean and easy to screenshot or share with teammates.
