# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Target User

Primary users:

- Junior to mid-level engineers at startups (10–200 employees)
- Founders managing small dev teams
- Indie hackers using multiple AI tools

They typically:

- Use 3–6 AI tools simultaneously
- Don’t track combined monthly AI spend properly
- Overpay for overlapping tools and plans

---

## User Behavior Before Finding This Tool

They usually:

- Notice rising monthly SaaS bills
- Feel unsure which AI tools are actually necessary
- Compare tools manually (if at all)
- Rely on guesswork instead of structured analysis

---

## Where They Exist Online

- Reddit: r/startups, r/Entrepreneur, r/programming
- Twitter/X: indie hackers, SaaS builders, devtool founders
- Discord communities: startup and AI builder groups
- Hacker News: “Show HN” and SaaS discussions

---

## Acquisition Strategy (First 100 Users)

1. Post on Hacker News as a free AI cost audit tool
2. Share breakdown examples on Twitter/X with real savings visuals
3. Engage in Reddit threads about “AI tool costs”
4. DM indie hackers offering free audits
5. Create shareable audit reports for viral distribution

---

## Unfair Advantage

Credex association gives credibility in AI tooling space, allowing trust-based distribution in early startup ecosystems.

---

## Success Metric (Week 1)

- 100–300 audits completed
- 20–40% email capture rate
- At least 5 users reporting meaningful savings insights
