# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Exact Target User

Not "startups." The specific person is:

**Engineering Manager or CTO at a 10–50 person B2B SaaS startup, Series A or late seed, US or UK based.**

More specifically:

- They approved 3+ AI tool subscriptions in the last 6 months
- They've never done a formal audit of overlapping usage
- They saw their AWS or Stripe bill and thought "we should look at our AI spend too"
- They are not the CFO — they don't have a formal procurement process — they just renew subscriptions by default

Secondary user: **Indie hacker or solo founder** running a side project with $100–500/month in AI tool spend who feels the cost but hasn't quantified it.

## How to Get the First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

**Week 1 — Seed with real audit examples**

1. Run the tool yourself on 3 realistic company profiles (5-person dev team, 20-person startup, solo indie hacker). Screenshot the results.
2. Post on Twitter/X: "I built a free tool that audits your AI tool spend. Here's what it found for a typical 10-person dev team: [screenshot]. Link in bio." — no fluff, lead with the number.
3. Post on Hacker News as Show HN: "Show HN: Free AI spend auditor for startups — finds plan mismatches and cheaper alternatives." Submit at 9am ET on a Tuesday or Wednesday.

**Week 2 — Community seeding**

4. Find 5 active threads on r/SaaS or r/startups where people mention AI tool costs. Comment with a genuinely useful reply, mention the tool naturally at the end.
5. Post in Lenny's Slack #tools: "Built a free tool to audit AI tool spend — curious if others find it useful. No signup required." One sentence. Link. Done.
6. DM 10 indie hackers on Twitter who have posted about their tool stack. Offer to run a free audit for them in exchange for honest feedback.

**Week 3 — Referral loop activation**

7. Every audit result has a share button. Add a line under it: "Know a founder overpaying for AI tools? Send them this report." The public URL does the work.
8. Reply to every person who shares or comments. Build in public. Post a "Day 14 update: X audits run, biggest saving found: $Y/month" tweet.

**Week 4 — Press and newsletters**

9. Submit to TLDR Tech, Superhuman newsletter, and Bytes.dev as a free tool launch. These reach 200,000+ developers combined. Free to submit.
10. Write one genuinely useful blog post: "The 6 most common ways startups overpay for AI tools (and what to do instead)." Post on Hashnode or Dev.to. Link to the tool.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

**Credex's existing customer base.**

Credex already sells discounted AI credits to startups. Every existing Credex customer is a perfect user for this tool — they're already spending on AI, already cost-conscious, and already trust Credex.

Sending one email to existing customers saying "we built a free audit tool — here's what it found for a company like yours" converts at a far higher rate than cold traffic. No other competitor can do this.
