# Economics

## What Is a Converted Lead Worth to Credex?

Credex sells discounted AI infrastructure credits — Cursor, Claude, ChatGPT Enterprise, and others. A customer who purchases through Credex typically buys credits in bulk.

**Estimating LTV (Lifetime Value):**

- Average startup AI tool spend: $500–$2,000/month
- Credex discount offered: 20–40% off retail
- Credex margin on resold credits: ~15–25%
- Average deal size (first purchase): $3,000–$8,000 in credits
- Gross margin per deal: ~$600–$1,500
- Repeat purchase rate: ~40% within 6 months
- Estimated LTV per converted customer: **$1,200–$3,000**

Conservative assumption used for modelling: **$1,500 LTV per converted customer.**

## Conversion Funnel Model

Working backwards from $1M ARR:

```
10,000 visitors/month
→ 3,500 audits completed (35% completion rate)
→ 1,050 emails captured (30% of completions)
→ 105 consultation calls booked (10% of captures — high-savings users only)
→ 26 credit purchases (25% close rate on calls)
→ $39,000 gross margin/month
→ $468,000 ARR at this funnel
```

To reach $1M ARR, scale to ~22,000 visitors/month or improve close rate to 35%.

**What makes these numbers realistic:**

- 35% audit completion is achievable for a no-login tool with immediate value
- 30% email capture is standard for post-value gating
- 10% consultation rate applies only to >$500/mo savings cases (~20% of audits)
- 25% close rate is conservative for warm, pre-qualified leads with demonstrated savings

## What Has to Be True for $1M ARR in 18 Months

| Assumption                               | Target   |
| ---------------------------------------- | -------- |
| Monthly visitors by month 6              | 8,000    |
| Monthly visitors by month 18             | 22,000   |
| Audit completion rate                    | ≥ 30%    |
| Email capture rate                       | ≥ 25%    |
| Consultation booking rate (high-savings) | ≥ 8%     |
| Sales close rate                         | ≥ 20%    |
| Average deal value                       | ≥ $4,000 |
| Customer repeat purchase rate            | ≥ 35%    |

The single biggest lever is **distribution** — the tool only works if founders find it at the moment they're questioning their AI spend. That moment is: reading a Hacker News thread about AI costs, seeing a tweet with a savings screenshot, or getting a share link from a teammate.

---

## Sensitivity Check

If close rate drops to 15% and deal size drops to $2,500:

```
26 purchases × 0.6 = ~16 purchases/month
16 × $2,500 × 20% margin = $8,000 gross margin/month
= $96,000 ARR
```

This means distribution and top-of-funnel volume matter more than close rate optimisation at this stage. Invest in channels first.

---

## Summary

The unit economics work at scale because:

1. CAC is near-zero in early organic channels
2. LTV ($1,500) is high relative to CAC ($8)
3. The tool pre-qualifies leads — users who see $500+/mo savings are already motivated buyers
4. The viral share loop (public audit URLs) compounds organic growth without paid spend
